# Tone Guidelines Reference

## Tone Markers and Characteristics

### Professional Tone
- **Word markers**: utilize, implement, appreciate, inform, scheduled, remains, pleased, regarding
- **Punctuation**: Periods throughout; no exclamation marks
- **Sentence length**: 15-20 words average
- **Pronouns**: Balanced use of "we" and "I" for accountability
- **Use case**: Business updates, formal requests, stakeholder communication
- **Example phrase**: "We appreciate your patience and are pleased to inform you that the project remains on track."

### Friendly Tone
- **Word markers**: use, try, thanks, happy, great, looking forward, love, enjoy, awesome
- **Punctuation**: Occasional exclamation marks (1 per 3-4 sentences)
- **Sentence length**: 10-15 words; shorter and punchier
- **Pronouns**: Natural use of "we" and "I"; conversational
- **Use case**: Team updates, casual collaboration, internal communication
- **Example phrase**: "Thanks so much for your help! We're really excited about the progress."

### Formal Tone
- **Word markers**: hereby, regarding, concerning, consequently, moreover, thus, furthermore, in accordance with
- **Punctuation**: Periods always; no contractions (don't → do not)
- **Sentence length**: Longer and more complex; varied structure
- **Pronouns**: Minimize personal pronouns; prefer passive voice
- **Use case**: Legal, official communications, formal announcements
- **Example phrase**: "It is hereby requested that all parties provide written confirmation of their approval."

### Apologetic Tone
- **Word markers**: sorry, sincerely apologize, understand, appreciate your patience, rectify, regret, commitment
- **Punctuation**: Periods; minimal exclamation marks
- **Sentence length**: Medium length; thoughtful pacing
- **Pronouns**: Frequent "I" (take responsibility); "we" for team involvement
- **Use case**: Addressing issues, recovery communications, apologies
- **Example phrase**: "I sincerely apologize for this delay and appreciate your understanding as we work to rectify the situation."

### Persuasive Tone
- **Word markers**: opportunity, advantage, recommend, suggest, consider, benefit, would benefit, suggest, compelling, strong
- **Punctuation**: Periods with strategic exclamation marks for impact (1 per 2-3 sentences)
- **Sentence length**: Mix of short declarative statements with explanations
- **Pronouns**: Balance "you" (benefit to reader) with "we" (team capability)
- **Use case**: Proposals, calls-to-action, sales communications
- **Example phrase**: "This opportunity would benefit our organization significantly by reducing operational costs by 30%."

## Word Substitution Examples

| Concept | Professional | Friendly | Formal | Apologetic | Persuasive |
|---------|--------------|----------|--------|------------|------------|
| **Happy/Good** | pleased | happy, great | satisfied | appreciate | excellent |
| **Complete** | scheduled, planned | gonna finish | shall be completed | finally done | accomplishment |
| **Request** | would appreciate | can you | hereby request | kindly advise | strongly recommend |
| **Problem** | challenge | issue, oops | concerning matter | regretfully | opportunity to improve |
| **Start** | initiate | start, kick off | commence | begin anew | launch |
| **Finish** | conclude | wrap up | complete | finally rectify | achieve |

## Checklist for Applying Tone

### Professional Tone Checklist
- [ ] No exclamation marks
- [ ] Word choices: "utilize", "inform", "appreciate", "remains"
- [ ] Sentences 15-20 words average
- [ ] Balanced "we" and "I"
- [ ] Formal closings: "Best regards", "Sincerely"

### Friendly Tone Checklist
- [ ] 1 exclamation mark per 3-4 sentences
- [ ] Word choices: "use", "thanks", "great", "looking forward"
- [ ] Sentences 10-15 words (punchy)
- [ ] Natural pronouns; conversational
- [ ] Casual closings: "Best", "Thanks", "Talk soon"

### Formal Tone Checklist
- [ ] No contractions (don't → do not)
- [ ] Word choices: "hereby", "concerning", "moreover"
- [ ] Longer, complex sentences
- [ ] Minimal pronouns; passive voice preferred
- [ ] Formal closings: "Sincerely", "Respectfully"

### Apologetic Tone Checklist
- [ ] "Sorry" or "sincerely apologize" early in email
- [ ] "Appreciate" + "patience" or similar
- [ ] "Commit" or "rectify" for solution focus
- [ ] Frequent "I" (responsibility); "we" (teamwork)
- [ ] Action-oriented language

### Persuasive Tone Checklist
- [ ] "Opportunity", "advantage", "benefit" present
- [ ] "Recommend" or "suggest" used
- [ ] Short strong statements + explanations
- [ ] "You" benefits highlighted
- [ ] Call to action clear

## Quick Reference: Tone Selection

**Choose Professional when:** Business communication, status updates, formal requests, stakeholder updates

**Choose Friendly when:** Team communication, internal updates, casual collaboration, colleague emails

**Choose Formal when:** Legal matters, official announcements, regulatory communication, contracts

**Choose Apologetic when:** Error/delay acknowledgment, recovery communication, addressing customer complaints

**Choose Persuasive when:** Proposals, budget requests, sales, calls to action, recommendations
